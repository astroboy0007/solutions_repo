Task 1

Functions:

  1. \( f(x) = x^2 \)
  2. \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \)
  3. \( h(x) = \frac{1}{x} \)
  4. \( j(x) = \sin(x) \)

Calculation:

For \( f(x) = x^2 \):

\[ f(2) = 2^2 = 4 \]

For \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \):

\[ g(2) = \sqrt{2} \approx 1.414 \]

For \( h(x) = \frac{1}{x} \):

\[ h(2) = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5 \]

For \( j(x) = \sin(x) \):

Assuming \( x = 2 \) is in radians,

\[ j(2) = \sin(2) \approx 0.909 \]

Summary: - \( f(2) = 4 \)
- \( g(2) \approx 1.414 \)
- \( h(2) = 0.5 \)
- \( j(2) \approx 0.909 \)

Task 2

Step 1: Definitions of the Functions Given:

  • \( f(x) = 3x - 1 \)
  • \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \)

Step 2: Compositions of Functions

  1. \( f(g(x)) \):

Substitute \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \) into \( f(x) \):

\[ f(g(x)) = f(\sqrt{x}) = 3\sqrt{x} - 1 \]
  1. \( g(f(x)) \):

Substitute \( f(x) = 3x - 1 \) into \( g(x) \):

\[ g(f(x)) = g(3x - 1) = \sqrt{3x - 1} \]
  1. \( f(f(x)) \):

Substitute \( f(x) = 3x - 1 \) into \( f(x) \) again:

\[ f(f(x)) = f(3x - 1) = 3(3x - 1) - 1 = 9x - 3 - 1 = 9x - 4 \]
  1. \( g(g(x)) \):

Substitute \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \) into \( g(x) \) again:

\[ g(g(x)) = g(\sqrt{x}) = \sqrt{\sqrt{x}} = x^{1/4} \]

Step 3: Instructions for Visualizing in GeoGebra To plot the functions \( f(g(x)) \), \( g(f(x)) \), \( f(f(x)) \), and \( g(g(x)) \) in GeoGebra:

  2. Access the GeoGebra application or the online version at GeoGebra.org.

  3. Enter the Functions:

    • \( g(f(x)) = \sqrt{3x - 1} \)

    • \( g(g(x)) = x^{1/4} \)

  5. Set the Domain:

  6. For \( f(g(x)) \), \( g(f(x)) \), and \( g(g(x)) \), restrict the domain to ensure the square root is defined (e.g., \( x \geq 0 \) for \( g(x) \)).

  7. Use the syntax in GeoGebra, such as:

  9. Assign different colors to each function for better visualization. Right-click on a function and select "Settings" to change its color and style.

  10. Analyze the Graphs:

  11. Use GeoGebra’s point tool to evaluate the compositions at specific \( x \)-values.
